# Engine RPM Diagnostics

## Normal Idle RPM
A healthy gasoline engine idles between 600 and 1000 RPM when warm.
Cold engines may idle higher (900-1200 RPM) during warm-up due to
enrichment strategies in the engine control unit.

## RPM and Vehicle Speed Correlation
Under normal driving conditions, RPM correlates strongly with vehicle speed
and gear selection. A high RPM reading (above 2500) combined with zero
vehicle speed and low throttle position is anomalous and may indicate:
- Vehicle stationary with engine revved (unusual driver behavior)
- Transmission slippage or clutch failure (manual transmissions)
- Faulty RPM sensor giving erroneous readings
- Data logging artifact during gear shifts

## High RPM Sustained Operation
RPM sustained above 4000 for extended periods, especially combined with
high engine coolant temperature, increases mechanical stress and can
indicate aggressive driving behavior or a driver unfamiliar with the
vehicle's optimal shift points.

## Redline and Rev Limiter
Most gasoline passenger vehicle engines have a redline (maximum safe RPM)
between 6000 and 7000 RPM, enforced electronically by a rev limiter.
Readings approaching this range should be rare in normal fleet operation.
