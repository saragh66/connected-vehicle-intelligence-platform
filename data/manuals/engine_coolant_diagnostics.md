# Engine Coolant Temperature Diagnostics

## Normal Operating Range
The typical engine coolant temperature for a gasoline engine at operating
temperature ranges between 80°C and 95°C. Temperatures below 70°C generally
indicate the engine has not yet reached operating temperature (cold start phase).

## Overheating Thresholds
- 95-100°C: Upper normal range, acceptable under heavy load or hot ambient conditions.
- 100-105°C: Warning zone. Sustained operation in this range accelerates wear
  on gaskets and can lead to coolant boil-over in non-pressurized systems.
- Above 105°C: Critical overheating. Risk of head gasket failure, warped
  cylinder head, or catastrophic engine damage. Immediate attention required.

## Common Causes of Overheating
1. Low coolant level due to leaks (hoses, radiator, water pump seal)
2. Failed thermostat stuck in closed position
3. Faulty radiator fan or fan clutch
4. Clogged radiator (external debris or internal scale buildup)
5. Water pump impeller failure or belt slippage
6. Blown head gasket allowing combustion gases into cooling system

## Cold Start Anomalies
Readings showing low coolant temperature (30-50°C) combined with low RPM
and zero vehicle speed typically indicate a cold start or idle-warm-up phase,
not a malfunction. This is a statistically rare but normal operating state.
